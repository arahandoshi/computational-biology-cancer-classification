# Biological Background

Cancer is a complex group of diseases characterized by uncontrolled cellular growth resulting from genetic and epigenetic alterations. Different cancer types exhibit unique molecular signatures that influence disease progression, prognosis, and response to therapy.

Gene expression profiling measures the activity of thousands of genes simultaneously and provides a comprehensive representation of cellular function. Advances in next-generation sequencing technologies have generated large-scale transcriptomic datasets that enable computational investigation of cancer biology.

Because gene expression datasets contain thousands of features and complex nonlinear relationships, traditional statistical approaches may struggle to identify meaningful patterns. Machine learning and deep learning methods provide powerful alternatives capable of discovering predictive relationships within high-dimensional biological data.

Accurate computational classification of cancer types has potential applications in precision medicine, diagnostic support, biomarker discovery, and personalized treatment planning. Consequently, cancer classification using gene expression data has become an important application area within computational biology, bioinformatics, and artificial intelligence.
